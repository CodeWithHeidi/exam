Source: Matt Beaumont. Mystips. https://mypstips.com/the-history-of-photoshop/

In the pivotal year of 1988, brothers Thomas and John Knoll introduced a groundbreaking software originally named "Display," which would later be known as Adobe Photoshop. Their initial goal was to create a tool for showcasing grayscale images on monochrome monitors; the Knoll brothers' ambitions quickly outgrew this modest objective. Thomas Knoll envisioned a more ambitious project, one capable of actively manipulating digital images. This vision materialized into Adobe Photoshop, a revolutionary software that transformed the digital imaging landscape. The Knoll brothers, driven by a shared passion for technology and a desire to push the boundaries of computer graphics, developed this pioneering tool. Adobe Photoshop emerged as a trailblazer, providing users with a comprehensive set of tools to manipulate, enhance, and transform digital images with unparalleled precision. Its evolution from a simple concept of displaying grayscale images to a sophisticated image-editing powerhouse highlights the inventiveness and foresight of its creators. Thomas and John Knoll's collaboration resulted in a tool that not only addressed the initial need for grayscale image display but also laid the groundwork for a fundamental shift in how digital images were perceived and manipulated. Adobe Photoshop's enduring impact reflects the innovative spirit of its inventors, who, in 1988, forever reshaped the landscape of digital creativity. The software's ability to provide image editing and empower users across various creative fields has solidified its place as an indispensable tool in the world of digital design and visual expression. With its user-friendly interface and unparalleled capabilities, Adobe Photoshop remains an enduring symbol of innovation in the field of digital imaging.
