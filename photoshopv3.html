In September 1994, Adobe released Adobe Photoshop 3.0, adding never before seen features

Source: Adobe. Adobe Photoshop 3.0. 1994. url:https://www.webdesignmuseum.org/old-software/graphic-software/adobe-photoshop-3-0

In the evolution of Adobe Photoshop, a significant milestone was reached with the release of version 3.0 in September 1994 for Mac OS 7, followed by the Windows version in November of the same year. This iteration marked a pivotal moment in the software's history, introducing a transformative featureâ€”Layers. The introduction of Layers revolutionized digital image editing by allowing users to work on different elements of an image independently. At the time, this feat was nothing short of a technological miracle, and made Adobe Photoshop incomparable to any other digital editing platform. This innovative feature provided a non-destructive way to manipulate and organize various components within a composition, granting users unprecedented control over their creative process. Prior to version 3.0, the absence of layer functionality posed limitations on the complexity and flexibility of image editing. The Mac OS 7 release followed by the Windows version demonstrated Adobe's commitment to providing a cross-platform experience, catering to a diverse user base. Photoshop 3.0 quickly became a game-changer for graphic designers, photographers, and artists, enabling them to craft more intricate and sophisticated compositions. Beyond the introduction of Layers, Photoshop 3.0 showcased other enhancements, including improved color management and additional filters, further solidifying its position as the go-to software for digital image manipulation. This release set the stage for Photoshop's continued dominance in graphic design and digital art, laying the foundation for subsequent versions that would build upon its legacy. The strategic release of Photoshop 3.0 in both Mac and Windows environments, coupled with the groundbreaking introduction of Layers, underscored Adobe's commitment to innovation and responsiveness to the evolving needs of its user community. This version not only marked a technological advancement but also played a pivotal role in shaping the creative landscape for years to come.
